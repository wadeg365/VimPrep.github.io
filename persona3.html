Focus & Priorities

As a Director of Data Engineering, Kunal will be deeply concerned with the technical architecture, data governance, and the engineering processes involved in the migration. His background in data modeling, data architecture, and cloud implementations (especially Azure) means he will be scrutinizing the proposed BigQuery Lakehouse architecture for its soundness, scalability, and maintainability.

He will also be interested in how the migration will impact his team's workflows and responsibilities, particularly considering the transition from their current environment to Google Cloud Platform.

Key Insight

Kunal's extensive experience in cloud migrations to Azure, building data warehouses, and implementing data governance programs makes him highly qualified to assess the technical aspects of this proposed migration to GCP. He will be evaluating the migration through the lens of someone who has led similar projects.

Watching Out For

  • The detailed technical architecture of the proposed BigQuery Lakehouse, including data storage, processing, and integration components.
  • The data modeling and schema design for the migrated data in BigQuery.
  • The plan for data governance, including master data management, data dictionary, and change management processes.
  • The ETL/ELT pipelines and data integration strategies for moving data from Snowflake and other sources to BigQuery.
  • The security measures and access controls within the BigQuery environment.
  • The operational aspects, such as monitoring, backup/restore, and disaster recovery plans for the new platform.
  • How the migration will impact his team's existing skills and the need for new expertise in GCP and BigQuery.

Potential Questions

"Can you provide a detailed logical and physical data model for the target BigQuery Lakehouse environment?"

Be prepared with comprehensive data models that show both the logical structure (entities, relationships) and physical implementation (tables, partitioning strategies, etc.) in BigQuery. Reference his experience creating such models to establish credibility.

"What specific GCP services will be leveraged for data storage, processing, and orchestration in the new architecture?"

Present a clear architecture diagram showing the GCP services ecosystem (BigQuery, Cloud Storage, Dataflow, Cloud Composer, etc.) and explain how they integrate. Compare with equivalent Azure services he may be familiar with.

"How will data governance be implemented in BigQuery, and how will we manage data quality, lineage, and metadata?"

Detail the data governance framework including Data Catalog for metadata management, data lineage tracking, and quality control processes. Emphasize how these solutions compare to or improve upon current governance practices.

"What is the proposed approach for migrating our existing ETL/ELT pipelines, and what tools will be used for this process?"

Outline the migration strategy for existing data pipelines, including assessment, translation to GCP equivalents (e.g., Dataflow), testing approach, and the phased transition plan to minimize disruption.

"How will security and access controls be managed in BigQuery to ensure data privacy and compliance?"

Explain BigQuery's security model, including IAM roles, row-level and column-level security, data masking capabilities, and integration with organizational security policies.

"What are the plans for operational support, monitoring, and disaster recovery for the new data platform?"

Present the operations framework including Cloud Monitoring dashboards, alerting systems, backup procedures, and disaster recovery capabilities built into the GCP infrastructure.

"What kind of training and resources will be available for our data engineering team to upskill on BigQuery and GCP technologies?"

Outline the comprehensive training plan, including official Google certifications, hands-on workshops, mentoring periods, and documentation that will support his team's transition from Azure to GCP.

Individual Wins

  • The implementation of a modern and scalable data platform based on BigQuery, aligning with current cloud best practices.
  • Improved data governance and data quality within the organization.
  • The opportunity to lead a significant cloud migration project and enhance his team's technical capabilities.
  • A more efficient and robust data infrastructure that reduces technical debt and supports future growth.

Success Scenario

For Kunal, success means implementing a technically sound, well-architected BigQuery Lakehouse that enhances his team's capabilities, provides better data governance, and establishes a platform that can scale with Vimeo's future needs. The migration gives him the opportunity to expand his cloud expertise beyond Azure into GCP, broadening his technical leadership profile.

Presentation Strategy Tips

Present Detailed Technical Architecture

Given his technical background, include comprehensive architecture diagrams and detailed technical specifications. Compare BigQuery capabilities with Azure Synapse Analytics and other services he's familiar with.

Address the Platform Transition

Acknowledge the shift from Azure to GCP and provide a clear comparison of equivalent services and concepts. Focus on how his team's existing skills will transfer and what new capabilities they will gain.

Emphasize Governance and Security

Given his experience in data governance, provide a thorough explanation of BigQuery's governance capabilities, including metadata management, data lineage, and security controls.

Detail the Migration Methodology

Present a phased migration approach with clear risk mitigation strategies. Include technical validation points, rollback procedures, and team skill development aspects of the migration plan.
